What is Connection Farmer?

Connection Farmer is a site designed for users to "farm" LinkedIn connections and track job search progress. If you have less than the preferred 500+ connections on your LinkedIn and want to change that, create a profile on this site and farm some connections with random, like-minded strangers!

How to Use Connection Farmer:

Simply sign up and create a profile to add yourself to the list of people open to farming connections. Then, you can go through the list of profiles, click on LinkedIn URLs, and send connection requests to as many people as you'd like.

How to Use Job Search Progress Tracker:

Once you've created a profile, you can add jobs you've applied to on your profile page. You can include details such as the kind of resume you submitted (general or a resume tailored to that specific job), your "fit"/how well you meet the job requirements, and your confidence in being hired.

Once you've registered a job, you can add an "event" via the jobs details page. This allows you to create a timeline of events such as "Applied", "Technical Interview", etc. You may also add how much time you spent on each event throughout the process. Connection Farmer will automatically calculate the total time you've spent on any given job application process or your total time spent searching for jobs, and it will display these figures on the relevant pages.

Why the farm theme?

Technopedia defines farming in a video game context to be a "tactic where a player, or someone hired by a player, performs repetitive actions to gain experience, points or some form of in-game currency. Farming usually involves staying in a game area with a spawn point that generates endless numbers of items or enemies."

So, in the context of this website, "farming" connections means performing the repetitive action of sending connection requests to others who show up at the "spawn point" (the profile list page) in order to make the process of getting the fabled "500+ Connections" milestone as efficient as possible.
